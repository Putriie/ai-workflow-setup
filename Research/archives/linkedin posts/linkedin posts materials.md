## 1. Samantha McKenna (@samsalesli)

**Background:** Founder & CEO of #samsales Consulting | Ex-LinkedIn executive | Creator of "Show Me You Know Me®" (SMYKM) methodology

---

### Post 1
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_samsales-activity-7313651755962040324-DghF
- **Date:** Early 2025
- **Summary:** McKenna shares her "Show Me You Know Me" (SMYKM) framework for cold outreach, explaining how hyper-personalized emails achieve open rates of 43% vs. the 6% industry average, with a 20% reply rate vs. 0.9% average.
- **Key Takeaway:** Generic outreach is dead. Personalizing your subject line AND body copy to demonstrate genuine research on the prospect — their company, role, and recent activity — drives 7x higher open rates.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Directly applicable to SaaS SDR and AE outbound motions. The SMYKM framework provides a replicable system for top-of-funnel personalization that scales without sacrificing authenticity.

---

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/samsalesli (Profile post — "Cold calling is dead" thread, referenced widely)
- **Date:** 2024–2025
- **Summary:** McKenna argues she has never made a cold call in 16 years of sales, building an 8-figure business solely on cold email. She outlines her 7 elements of the perfect cold email: subject line with SMYKM, relevant opener, value proposition, social proof, objection handling, clear CTA, and appropriate length.
- **Key Takeaway:** Quality beats quantity. Cold emails built on deep research and purpose-built value props outperform high-volume spray-and-pray sequences every time.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Ideal for SaaS companies where buyers are flooded with AI-generated outreach. McKenna's framework helps reps break through noise by treating each prospect as an individual — especially critical in enterprise SaaS sales.

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benyaminholley_just-saw-samantha-mckenna-tell-everyone-that-activity-7311774658666655746-a5gq
- **Date:** March 2025 (referenced in this post)
- **Summary:** McKenna publicly challenges the industry norm of cold calling, sparking broad debate. She doubles down on written outreach as the superior channel, citing her consulting firm's results across 250+ SaaS and technology clients.
- **Key Takeaway:** Channel selection matters. For certain ICP segments (senior execs, technical buyers), well-crafted written outreach outperforms cold calls — and McKenna's data from real SaaS client engagements backs this up.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Challenges SDR teams to rethink their channel mix and invest more in written personalization, particularly for high-ACV SaaS deals where reputation and first impressions carry more weight.

## 2. Josh Braun (@josh-braun)

**Background:** Sales trainer, author of "Badass B2B Growth Guide" | Teaches non-pushy, buyer-centric prospecting

---

### Post 1
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_prospecting-is-like-throwing-a-party-you-activity-6957843051335540738-NsoC
- **Date:** July 2022
- **Summary:** Braun uses the metaphor of throwing a party to reframe SDR anxiety around prospecting outcomes. You control who you invite, what you say, and how you show up — but not who responds.
- **Key Takeaway:** Detach from outcomes and focus on the craft of outreach. Reps who obsess over reply rates burn out; reps who focus on improving their process consistently win long-term.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Critical mindset shift for SaaS SDR teams where rejection is constant. Helps build a sustainable, high-volume outbound culture without the emotional attrition that kills pipeline productivity.

---

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_josh-a-prospect-responded-to-my-cold-email-activity-7074138982707556352-T9Cm
- **Date:** June 2023
- **Summary:** A rep asks Braun how to handle "not interested right now, will reach out when ready." Braun explains this isn't an objection — it's simply not the right moment. Using Jobs-to-be-Done theory, he reframes the SDR's role as planting seeds, not forcing urgency.
- **Key Takeaway:** Most prospects aren't in buy mode when you reach out cold. The goal is to be memorable and helpful enough that when they enter the buying window, you're top of mind — not to force a meeting that isn't ready.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Especially relevant for SaaS products with long consideration cycles (6–18 months). Braun's nurture-first philosophy helps teams avoid burning bridges and damaging ICP relationships with pushy follow-ups.

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_inbound-vs-outbound-starting-the-conversation-activity-7280213681483788288-puKF
- **Date:** January 2025
- **Summary:** Braun contrasts inbound vs. outbound conversation starters, arguing that outbound requires fundamentally different openers because prospects haven't raised their hand. He provides frameworks for starting cold conversations without triggering the prospect's "sales alert."
- **Key Takeaway:** Outbound openers must lead with insight or curiosity — not product pitches. The best cold outreach makes the prospect feel understood before it ever mentions what you sell.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Directly applicable to SaaS AE self-sourcing and SDR sequences. Reframes the first touchpoint as a conversation-starter rather than a pitch — reducing prospect resistance significantly.

- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Foundational for SaaS sales leadership. Helps VPs and CROs align team priorities around top-of-funnel activity rather than just closing — which is especially critical when inbound leads slow down.

- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Directly relevant to SaaS revenue leaders looking to improve pipeline efficiency. Encourages SDR→AE handoff quality and full-cycle accountability rather than siloed metrics.

## 4. Kyle Coleman (@kyletcoleman)

**Background:** Global VP Marketing @ ClickUp | Former VP Revenue Growth @ Clari | Vocal advocate for personalization over automation

---

### Post 1
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyletcoleman_sdr-outbound-prospecting-activity-6631211778837749761-LZqp
- **Date:** 2020 (evergreen, widely shared)
- **Summary:** Coleman tracked every cold outreach touchpoint he received over 10 days: 82 templated emails (zero responses), 2 personalized emails (both responses); 48 templated LinkedIn messages (zero responses), 10 personalized (most responses). Direct mail had a 100% response rate.
- **Key Takeaway:** The data is unambiguous — templates get ignored, personalization gets responses. SDRs who invest in research and custom messaging at a lower volume consistently outperform high-volume automation.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** A wake-up call for SaaS SDR teams relying on mass sequencing tools. The post demonstrates quantitatively that multi-channel, personalized outreach is the only strategy that earns responses from senior decision-makers.

---

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/kyletcoleman (2025 profile post on SDR systems)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Coleman argues SDRs don't need more motivation — they need better systems. He outlines the winning 2025 SDR setup: clear ICP + messaging, zero manual busywork (automated infrastructure), and automation of everything except the human conversation layer.
- **Key Takeaway:** The winning formula for outbound in 2025 is: eliminate all non-human tasks through automation, then focus rep energy exclusively on research, personalization, and live conversation.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Highly relevant for SaaS RevOps and sales enablement leaders building or rebuilding SDR programs. Separates tool infrastructure (automatable) from human judgment (irreplaceable).

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyletcoleman_the-best-two-cold-emails-ive-gotten-so-activity-6770013018194632704-KRM2
- **Date:** February 2021
- **Summary:** Coleman shares the two best cold emails he's received (and agreed to take meetings from), breaking down exactly why they worked: both led with a specific, relevant insight about him or his company rather than a product pitch.
- **Key Takeaway:** The best cold emails feel less like sales and more like a colleague sharing a valuable observation. Leading with genuine insight — not a hook, not a compliment — is the fastest path to a meeting.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Provides a real-world benchmark for SaaS reps writing cold emails to senior marketing and revenue leaders. Coleman's examples are often referenced as gold-standard B2B outreach templates.

- **Date:** October 2023
- **Summary:** Bay shares a mobile-first cold email framework — emails under 75 words, short paragraphs, and punchy subject lines — based on the insight that most B2B buyers now read email on mobile, and long emails are dismissed before the value is ever communicated.
- **Key Takeaway:** Format is as important as content. Cold emails written for desktop fail on mobile. SDRs should write every cold email assuming it'll be read on a phone screen in 8 seconds.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Immediately actionable for SaaS SDR teams. Mobile-optimizing cold emails doesn't require new tools — just new writing discipline. High impact, low cost change that can improve response rates quickly.

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_sales-prospecting-outbound-activity-7095374466934808576-7r1R
- **Date:** August 2023
- **Summary:** Bay discusses cold call pick-up rates (4–5% average), sharing practical timing tips: call Monday mornings and Friday afternoons (when fewer reps are calling), dial direct lines and cell numbers, and call 5 minutes before/after the top of the hour.
- **Key Takeaway:** Outbound cold calling is a timing and channel game. Small tactical changes to when and where you call can dramatically improve connect rates without requiring any change to your pitch.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Practical and immediately deployable for SaaS SDR teams. In an era where phone is often deprioritized, Bay's tactical advice helps teams extract maximum ROI from their cold calling motion.

## 6. Steli Efti (@steli-efti)

**Background:** CEO of Close.io (CRM) | Author of "The Startup Sales Playbook" | Known for relentless follow-up philosophy

---

### Post 1
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/steli-efti (Close.io blog cross-post on follow-up sequences)
- **Date:** 2024–2025
- **Summary:** Efti makes the case for breakup emails as a highly effective but underused tactic. Rather than letting dead prospects sit in sequences indefinitely, a well-crafted breakup email often revives conversations — because it signals respect for the prospect's time.
- **Key Takeaway:** The breakup email is not a last resort — it's a strategic tool. Sending a respectful, non-pushy "this is my last reach out" message often gets more responses than the previous 4–5 touchpoints combined.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Directly applicable to SaaS outbound sequences. Many SDR teams over-extend sequences without a clean breakup. Efti's approach provides a professional close to sequences that preserves brand reputation while occasionally reviving warm leads.

---

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/steli-efti (Startup sales founder-led cold outreach)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Efti argues that for early-stage SaaS startups, founders must do the first 100 cold outreach conversations themselves before hiring SDRs. This is because founders carry the authentic "why" that no SDR script can replicate — and it informs all future messaging.
- **Key Takeaway:** Founder-led selling is a research phase, not a pride phase. The intelligence gathered from personally doing cold outreach informs ICP clarity, messaging, and the eventual SDR playbook.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Particularly valuable for pre-SDR B2B SaaS companies or those rebuilding their GTM. Efti's philosophy directly challenges the instinct to outsource outbound too early and lose the learning loop.

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/steli-efti (Follow up until you get a yes or no)
- **Date:** 2024
- **Summary:** Efti shares his "follow up until you get a yes or a no" philosophy — arguing that most salespeople give up after 2–3 touches, while data shows most closes happen between touches 5–12.
- **Key Takeaway:** Persistence — done respectfully and with added value at each touch — is a competitive advantage. The reps who follow up the most thoughtfully, not the most aggressively, win more deals.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Particularly relevant for SaaS deals with longer sales cycles. Efti's follow-up philosophy, when combined with value-adding touchpoints (case studies, relevant content, product updates), keeps SaaS pipeline warm without being annoying.

- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** High-relevance for SaaS SDR teams targeting mid-market and enterprise buyers. Video prospecting scales well with tools like Loom and Vidyard, and aligns with the SaaS industry's comfort with async communication.

- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Particularly valuable for SaaS RevOps leaders and CROs. Altschuler's portfolio-level perspective provides rare insight into what's actually working across multiple high-growth SaaS companies simultaneously.

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/maxaltschuler (AI-powered outbound: hype vs. reality)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Altschuler addresses the AI outbound revolution, arguing that while AI has made high-volume outreach cheap, it has also made it commoditized. The edge now belongs to teams that use AI for research and analysis — not for writing generic emails at scale.
- **Key Takeaway:** AI should power the research layer of outbound (finding signals, summarizing prospect context), not the writing layer. AI-written cold emails are now the lowest common denominator — easy to spot, easy to delete.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Highly relevant for SaaS sales teams evaluating AI SDR tools. Altschuler's nuanced take helps teams avoid the trap of automating themselves into irrelevance while still leveraging AI for competitive advantage.

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/maxaltschuler (Sales Hacker Newsletter — outbound best practices 2025)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Altschuler shares the A/B testing framework he still uses from his Sales Hacker days: test subject lines first, then opening lines, then CTAs. Never change more than one variable per test, and run each test for a minimum of 100 sends before drawing conclusions.
- **Key Takeaway:** A/B testing cold email is non-negotiable for SaaS teams serious about outbound optimization. Small improvements in subject line performance compound dramatically across thousands of sequences.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Immediately actionable for SaaS sales ops and enablement teams. Altschuler's structured A/B testing approach can be implemented in any major sequencing tool (Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo) without additional cost.

## 9. Armand Farrokh (@armand-farrokh)

**Background:** Host of "30 Minutes to President's Club" podcast | VP of Sales coach | Known for tactical, framework-heavy sales content

---

### Post 1
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_30mpc-sales-sdr-activity-6854096838383943681-Ggbt
- **Date:** October 2021 (evergreen framework)
- **Summary:** Farrokh introduces the "3x3 rule" for cold emails: no more than 3 body sections, each no longer than 3 lines. The structure flows: Problem → Social Proof → CTA. Anything longer loses the reader before the value lands.
- **Key Takeaway:** Cold email brevity is not a style choice — it's a conversion tactic. Respecting the prospect's time by being ruthlessly concise signals professionalism and increases the likelihood they'll respond.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** One of the most-referenced frameworks in SaaS SDR training. The 3x3 rule is easy to teach, easy to audit, and directly improves deliverability and reply rates when applied consistently.

---

### Post 2
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_sales-sdr-coldcalling-activity-6901952603966599168-h_gF
- **Date:** 2022
- **Summary:** Farrokh shares how to handle early cold call objections ("not interested") by asking "what's happening in 6 months?" instead of pushing for an immediate meeting. This reframes the conversation around the prospect's future buying window rather than the rep's current quota.
- **Key Takeaway:** When a prospect says "not interested," the best move isn't to overcome the objection — it's to understand their timeline. Qualifying future intent is far more valuable than forcing a premature meeting.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Essential for SaaS SDRs and AEs calling into accounts with 3–12 month buying cycles. Farrokh's technique transforms cold call rejections into pipeline intelligence that informs future nurture strategy.

---

### Post 3
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/armand-farrokh (SDR mock cold call hiring test, 2025)
- **Date:** May 2025
- **Summary:** Farrokh argues that the mock cold call is the best SDR hiring test by far — and explains that the call itself matters less than how the candidate handles the debrief. He sends candidates a podcast episode to prepare, then evaluates their ability to learn, adapt, and self-correct.
- **Key Takeaway:** The best predictor of SDR success is coachability, not natural talent. Building cold call roleplays into the hiring process identifies which candidates will improve their outreach over time — the only ones worth hiring.
- **Relevance to B2B SaaS:** Critical for SaaS sales leaders building or scaling SDR teams. Farrokh's hiring framework helps identify reps who will compound their outbound performance rather than plateau — directly impacting pipeline quality and volume long-term.
