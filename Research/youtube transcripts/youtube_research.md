## 1. Samantha McKenna (#samsales)

**Background:** Founder of #samsales Consulting, ex-LinkedIn executive, creator of the "Show Me You Know Me" (SMYKM) framework. Achieves reported 43% open rates and 20% reply rates on cold email.

---

### Video 1: Show Me You Know Me: Mastering Personalization in Sales Outreach
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IfxqQWdMDYI
- **Published:** June 18, 2024
- **Why it's valuable:** This is McKenna's definitive breakdown of the SMYKM methodology — her core intellectual framework for hyper-personalized cold outreach that has driven industry-leading open rates. It goes far beyond "add their name to the email."
- **Key Lessons:**
  - The subject line must be so specific that only the recipient would understand it — referencing a unique personal or professional data point (a club they belong to, a post they made, a recent hire)
  - The email body should anticipate and neutralize the most likely objection before the prospect can raise it
  - Value prop framing: describe the problem you solve, not the product you sell
  - Research depth matters — spend more time on fewer, higher-quality prospects rather than blasting volume
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** Directly applicable to enterprise SaaS outreach where decision-makers receive hundreds of templated emails. The SMYKM approach differentiates your sequence from AI-generated noise and opens doors to C-suite and VP-level buyers.

---

### Video 2: Show Me You Know Me – Use Data To Personalize Your Outreach + Build Customer Relationships
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pzY4QlG-X5A
- **Published:** June 3, 2023
- **Why it's valuable:** Focuses specifically on *how* to find and operationalize personalization data at scale — bridging the gap between the SMYKM philosophy and its practical execution using LinkedIn Sales Navigator and public signals.
- **Key Lessons:**
  - Use LinkedIn activity (posts, comments, shares) as raw material for subject lines and openers
  - Trigger-based outreach: funding rounds, executive hires, product launches, and job postings are high-signal moments to reach out
  - Personalization should connect your value prop to the prospect's current context — not just flatter them
  - Builds a "personalization at scale" workflow that doesn't require hours per email
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** Provides a repeatable research-to-outreach process that SDR teams can systematize using intent data and Sales Navigator. Directly feeds into building an account-based outreach pipeline.

---

### Video 3: Don't BURY THE LEAD in Your Cold Emails with Sam McKenna
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HTknfYSLeEM
- **Published:** July 28, 2022
- **Why it's valuable:** McKenna diagnoses the single most common structural failure in cold emails — burying the core value proposition in paragraph three after a long preamble. Sharp, compact, and immediately applicable.
- **Key Lessons:**
  - The first sentence of your email should be the thing the prospect cares most about — not an intro about yourself
  - "Manners-first" selling: respect the prospect's time by being direct and clear about what you want and why
  - Cold email is not a pitch deck — strip everything that doesn't serve the reader
  - The CTA should be low-friction and specific, not open-ended
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** Most SaaS cold emails open with feature lists or company backstory. McKenna's "lead with the problem" structure maps perfectly to how SaaS buyers evaluate new tools: does this solve my pain, fast?

## 2. Josh Braun

**Background:** Founder of Josh Braun Sales Training, author of "Badass B2B Growth Guide." Known for his "radically honest," non-manipulative approach to cold outreach where prospects feel safe telling the truth.

---

### Video 1: How to Overcome Prospect Resistance: Josh Braun 4T Cold Email Framework
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cKZVyAQ3mY
- **Published:** April 17, 2024
- **Why it's valuable:** Introduces Braun's 4T framework (Trigger, Think, Third-Party Credibility, Talk), which is one of the most structured and psychologically grounded cold email templates in circulation. Teaches the underlying *why* behind each element.
- **Key Lessons:**
  - **Trigger:** Open with a specific observation about the prospect that makes the email feel researched, not spammed
  - **Think:** Plant a thought or reframe how they see a problem they already have — don't lead with your solution
  - **Third-Party Credibility:** Reference a customer, stat, or case study that neutralizes skepticism without sounding boastful
  - **Talk:** End with a low-pressure, honest ask — often a question, not a meeting request
  - "Prospect resistance" is reduced when emails feel like they come from someone who genuinely understands the reader's world
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** The 4T framework is directly usable for SaaS SDR teams writing sequences. It produces emails that don't feel like templates even when they follow one.

---

### Video 2: How to Create and Scale a Cold Email Prospecting Strategy (Sales Leadership Show)
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vdtLIUmGkXs
- **Published:** February 21, 2021
- **Why it's valuable:** One of Braun's most comprehensive public interviews, covering not just email copy but the full prospecting architecture — from ICP definition to sequencing logic to how to test and iterate.
- **Key Lessons:**
  - Start with a narrow, well-defined ICP before writing a single email — relevance beats volume every time
  - "Chum the waters" before outreach using social touches (engaging with their content) to warm up cold prospects
  - Sequences should vary the medium (email, phone, LinkedIn, video) — not just resend the same email
  - Measure reply rate, not open rate — opens are vanity; replies are pipeline
  - The goal of cold outreach is to start a conversation, not close a deal
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** Provides the strategic architecture for building a scalable outbound engine at a SaaS company, including how to design sequences that feel human across multiple touchpoints.

---

### Video 3: Let's Write a Good Cold Email
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lVUDHxrZJCY
- **Published:** October 11, 2023
- **Why it's valuable:** Live, unscripted email writing exercise where Braun builds a cold email from scratch in real time — extremely educational because you see the thinking and editing process, not just the finished product.
- **Key Lessons:**
  - Good cold emails read like they were written to one person, not 1,000
  - Ruthlessly edit: remove every word that doesn't add value for the reader
  - Avoid corporate filler language ("synergies," "best-in-class," "I wanted to reach out") — replace with specifics
  - End with curiosity, not urgency — "Is this worth a 15-minute conversation?" outperforms "Book a demo now"
  - The editing pass is as important as the writing pass
- **B2B SaaS Relevance:** Teaches the craft of cold email writing in a way that reps can replicate immediately. Particularly useful for SDR onboarding and team training.
